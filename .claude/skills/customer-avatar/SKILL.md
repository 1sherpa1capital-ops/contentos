## Purpose

Create a psychologically precise customer avatar that drives targeting, messaging, and offer decisions. This is not a generic persona — it's a specific human being with specific fears, desires, and language patterns that make your marketing feel like mind-reading.

**See prompts/custavagen.md** for the base interview methodology.

## Core Philosophy

### One Specific Person, Not a Demographic

A demographic is "marketing agency founders 30-45." An avatar is "George, 37, runs an 8-person agency in Austin, hasn't slept properly in 3 weeks because leads go cold overnight, and his wife mentioned last week that she misses seeing him before 10pm."

### Specific Over Generic

| Generic (WRONG) | Specific (RIGHT) |
|----------------|------------------|
| "They want to save time" | "They want to stop writing proposals at 10pm after client work" |
| "They're busy" | "They work 55 hours/week and haven't vacationed in 2 years" |
| "They fear losing clients" | "They lost a $15K client from a 6-hour response delay" |

### Verbatim Language Is Non-Negotiable

The language bank is the most valuable section. These are exact phrases the customer uses. When you use their words back to them, it creates a "you get me" moment.

Bad: "Our customers want AI automation solutions."
Good: "My leads go cold while I'm sleeping. That's $3K-5K of pipeline gone every single night."

## Self-Check Checklist (From evals/avatar-evals.md)

Before finalizing, verify your avatar passes these 5 binary criteria:

- [ ] **Specific Fear:** Name specific fear, not generic anxiety (e.g., "losing $15K client from 6-hour delay" not "fear of losing clients")
- [ ] **Verbatim Language:** Include phrases they actually say (marketing-speak like "maximize ROI" fails)
- [ ] **Before/After State:** Map clear before/after (what life looks like now vs. after)
- [ ] **Trigger Event:** Identify specific trigger (what made them search TODAY)
- [ ] **One Person:** Avatar is one specific person, recognizable in a room of 100

If any check fails, revise that section.

## Constraints

1. **NEVER use vague terms** — "many people," "often," "typically" are forbidden
2. **ALWAYS include concrete numbers** — ages, timelines, dollar amounts, hours lost
3. **NEVER skip any template section** — Every field must be filled
4. **Use psychological inference** — Fill gaps with evidence-based patterns
5. **Verbatim phrases must sound human** — If it sounds like marketing copy, it's wrong
